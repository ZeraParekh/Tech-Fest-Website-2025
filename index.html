Importance of Trees

By Zera Trees are the lungs of our land

Why Trees Matter?

Trees are essential for life on Earth. They give us oxygen, food, shade, and help fight climate change.

Benefits

  • Clean the air by removing pollution like nitrogen dioxide.
  • Fight climate change by storing carbon dioxide.
  • Provide shelter, nesting, and food for many animals, birds, and insects.
  • Protect soil from being washed away by rain and make the soil healthier.
  • Help manage water by filtering rain and preventing floods.
  • Keep cities cool by providing shade and releasing water vapor.
  • Give us materials, support health, and make neighborhoods look better.

What Can You Do?

  • Plant trees at home or join a community planting event.
  • Reuse, Recycle and Reduce
  • Water young trees correctly to help them survive during dry times.
  • Talk to friends and family about why saving forests is important.
  • Volunteer or donate to groups that plant and care for trees in your area.
  • Learn to identify local tree types and what they contribute to the environment.

Why do plants need only a specific amount of water?

It enables fundamental processes like photosynthesis and transpiration. Not only that but it supports nutrient uptake and prevents stress from both drought and over-saturation.
